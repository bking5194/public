Infinte 3-Card Poker is a spin on 3-Card Poker where the player and house draw from an infinite deck of cards. These games are very similar. Both the house and player draw 3 cards, The house must draw a Queen high hand to qualify, the betting pattern is the same, and the Ante and Pair+ bet are seperate.

However, there are some key differences to understand.

  • The hand rankings are different. Some draws have become more rare while others have become more common. For instance, A straight now beats a flush.
  • Hand comparison are different. When comparing your hand with the house hand, you only need to consider the hand type (pair,flush,etc) and the highest active card. For example, the hand AK3 is equal to the hand AK2 because they are both high card hands with the highest card being an Ace. This also means that the straight A23 is equal to the straight AKQ.
  • A Tie means a victory for the player. (Dont know if this is different from regualr 3-Card Poker)
  • When the house doesn't qualify or the player wins, the Ante pays out 2:1 (1:1 in regular 3-Card Poker)
